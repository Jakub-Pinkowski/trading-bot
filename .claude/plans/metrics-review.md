# Metrics Review

Covers all metrics produced by `per_trade_metrics.py` and `summary_metrics.py`, with observations
from the live CSV output and the aggregation logic in `strategy_analyzer.py`.

---

## Legend

- **Fine** — correct, interval-independent, no issues
- **Debatable** — valid concept but has caveats worth knowing
- **Broken/Misleading** — produces wrong or incomparable values in current form
- **Missing** — not computed but would be useful

---

## Per-Trade Metrics (`per_trade_metrics.py`)

| Metric                                                         | Status    | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|----------------------------------------------------------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `entry_time`, `exit_time`, `entry_price`, `exit_price`, `side` | Fine      | Raw trade data, no issues                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| `duration_hours`                                               | Fine      | Correct per-trade. Problem arises in *summary* (see below)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| `net_pnl`                                                      | Fine      | Gross P&L minus fixed $4 commission. Commission is flat regardless of contract size or slippage — acceptable simplification                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| `return_percentage_of_contract`                                | Fine      | `net_pnl / (entry_price × multiplier) × 100`. Consistent within a symbol. Problematic when aggregated across symbols with different contract values — a 0.1% move on ZS ($22k contract) ≠ 0.1% on CL ($70k contract) in capital terms                                                                                                      |
| `return_percentage_of_margin`                                  | Debatable | Correct concept — normalizes by actual capital at risk. But margin ratios are fixed class-level estimates from Jan 2026, applied to all historical periods. Margin requirements change with volatility. More honest than contract-based for cross-symbol comparison. **Currently calculated but never surfaced in summary metrics output** |
| `margin_requirement`                                           | Debatable | Derived from fixed asset-class ratios. Acceptable for relative comparison, misleading as an absolute dollar figure for historical trades                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| `commission`                                                   | Debatable | Flat $4 per trade regardless of contract size. A $4 commission on a $70k CL trade (0.006%) vs a $4 commission on a $5k MZC trade (0.08%) creates inconsistent cost modelling across symbols                                                                                                                                                |

## Summary Metrics (`summary_metrics.py`)

### Basic Trade Statistics

| Metric                                | Status                | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|---------------------------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `total_trades`                        | Fine                  |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| `winning_trades` / `losing_trades`    | Fine                  | Counts, not percentages — fine                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| `win_rate`                            | Fine                  | Interval-independent ratio                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| `average_trade_duration_hours`        | **Broken/Misleading** | The core TODO. A 4-bar trade on 4h = 16 hours; a 4-bar trade on 15m = 1 hour. When aggregated across intervals in the CSV, the average blends these (CSV shows values like 440h, 576h). Cross-interval comparison is meaningless. **Fix: add `average_trade_duration_bars` = duration normalised by interval length** |
| `total_wins_percentage_of_contract`   | Debatable             | Useful as an intermediate for profit_factor, but as a standalone metric it grows with trade count. Surfacing it in output is noise — it's only meaningful relative to `total_losses`                                                                                                                                  |
| `total_losses_percentage_of_contract` | Debatable             | Same as above                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |

### Return Metrics

| Metric                                        | Status                | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
|-----------------------------------------------|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `total_return_percentage_of_contract`         | **Broken/Misleading** | Accumulates with trade frequency. A 15m strategy over the same date range produces ~4× more trades than a 4h strategy — its total return is inflated purely by frequency. Not comparable across intervals. In the CSV, `Total Return %` ranges from +48% to -242%, making rank ordering unreliable. **Fix: normalize by bars or time**                                             |
| `average_trade_return_percentage_of_contract` | **Bug (minor)**       | Computed as `safe_average([total_return], total_trades)` → `total_return / total_trades`. Gives the correct number but is misleading code — it passes a single-element list with an explicit count. Should be `safe_average(self.returns)`. The result equals `(win_rate × avg_win) + (loss_rate × avg_loss)` which is the classic **expectancy** — fine metric if correctly named |
| `average_win_percentage_of_contract`          | Fine                  | Per-trade average, interval-independent                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| `average_loss_percentage_of_contract`         | Fine                  | Per-trade average, interval-independent                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| `profit_factor`                               | Fine                  | `total_wins% / abs(total_losses%)`. Interval-independent ratio. Best single summary metric in the set. Infinity replaced with 9999.99 — fine for aggregation                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |

### Risk Metrics

| Metric                        | Status                | Notes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `maximum_drawdown_percentage` | Debatable             | Calculated on the cumulative trade-return sequence, not on a time series. A 15m strategy with 400 trades has more opportunities for consecutive drawdown than a 4h strategy with 100 trades over the same period. Drawdown calculation starts peak at 0 — correct (treats initial equity as peak). Cross-interval comparison is unreliable                                                                                                                                  |
| `sharpe_ratio`                | **Broken/Misleading** | Computed as `avg_per_trade_return / std_dev(per_trade_returns)`. Standard Sharpe uses **time-period returns** (daily/monthly), not per-trade returns. A strategy with 400 trades has a fundamentally different "unit of time" than one with 100 trades. The ratio is not comparable across intervals or trade frequencies. Also uses `ddof=0` (population std dev) instead of `ddof=1` (sample std dev), which is technically wrong for a sample of trades                  |
| `sortino_ratio`               | **Broken/Misleading** | Same time-unit problem as Sharpe. Additionally, the denominator uses downside deviation calculated over **only the negative returns** (`downside_variance = avg([r² for r in negative_returns])`), but the standard Sortino formula divides the sum of squared negative deviations by **all N returns**, not just the negative count. This inflates the Sortino ratio when losses are rare. Example: 10% loss rate → denominator is 10× too small → Sortino is 10× too high |
| `calmar_ratio`                | **Broken/Misleading** | Industry Calmar = **annualised** return / max drawdown. This implementation uses **total cumulative** return, which grows with trade count and dataset length. A strategy with 2× more trades appears 2× better by Calmar even with identical per-trade performance. Not comparable across intervals or dataset lengths                                                                                                                                                     |
| `value_at_risk`               | Debatable             | Historical VaR on per-trade returns at 95% confidence. Valid concept per-trade. Main issues: (1) `MIN_RETURNS_FOR_VAR = 5` is far too low — with N=5, `index = int(0.05×5) = 0`, so VaR = worst single trade, not a 95th-percentile estimate; (2) not time-normalised, not comparable across intervals                                                                                                                                                                      |
| `expected_shortfall`          | Debatable             | Same caveats as VaR. With N=5: takes average of worst 2 trades. With N=20: index=1, takes average of worst 2 trades (10th percentile, not 5th). The `max(1, index+1)` floor means ES always covers at least 2 trades, but the effective confidence level varies with N                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| `ulcer_index`                 | Debatable             | Measures depth × duration of drawdowns in trade-sequence space. Same cross-interval incompatibility as max drawdown. Within a single (symbol, interval) run it's meaningful — penalises strategies that stay underwater for many consecutive trades                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |

## Aggregation Issues (`strategy_analyzer.py`)

These are problems that arise when combining results across symbols/intervals:

| Issue                                                                                                 | Detail                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `average_trade_duration_hours` is simple-averaged across combinations                                 | Already broken per combination; averaging broken values compounds the problem                                                                                                                                                                             |
| `total_return_percentage_of_contract` is **summed** across combinations                               | A strategy tested on 4 symbols gets 4× the total return of the same strategy on 1 symbol — not a fair comparison unless you intentionally want "total across all capital deployed"                                                                        |
| `sharpe_ratio` / `sortino_ratio` / `calmar_ratio` are **trade-weighted averaged** across combinations | These should be recalculated from scratch on the combined trade stream. Averaging a Sharpe of 0.5 (100 trades) and a Sharpe of 1.5 (100 trades) gives 1.0, but recalculating from the combined 200 trades would give a different and more accurate number |
| Simple (non-weighted) averaging treats a 10-trade combo equal to a 100-trade combo                    | Min-trades filter helps, but the weighting problem remains for strategies at the boundary                                                                                                                                                                 |

## Proposed New Metrics

| Metric                                   | Formula                                                    | Rationale                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| `average_trade_duration_bars`            | `duration_hours / interval_hours` per trade, then averaged | Fixes the core TODO. A 4-bar trade is a 4-bar trade regardless of interval. Directly comparable across 15m / 1h / 4h                                                                                           |
| `win_loss_ratio`                         | `avg_win / abs(avg_loss)`                                  | Classic complement to win_rate. High win_rate + low W/L ratio = marginal strategy. Already computable from existing metrics but not surfaced. Together with win_rate it fully describes the trade distribution |
| `expectancy_per_bar`                     | `average_trade_return / average_trade_duration_bars`       | Return earned per bar held. Normalises both for trade size and for time, making cross-interval comparison meaningful. Answers: "how much does each candle of holding contribute on average?"                   |
| `max_consecutive_losses`                 | Longest losing streak in trade sequence                    | Practical risk metric — determines minimum account size needed to survive drawdowns. Not captured by max_drawdown alone                                                                                        |
| `max_consecutive_wins`                   | Longest winning streak                                     | Useful for detecting mean-reversion vs trend-following behaviour                                                                                                                                               |
| `return_percentage_of_margin` in summary | Already computed per-trade, just not aggregated            | More honest than contract-based for cross-symbol capital efficiency comparison. Especially important when mixing high-margin assets (crypto 40%) with low-margin ones (forex 4%)                               |
| `time_in_market_percentage`              | `sum(duration_hours) / total_dataset_hours × 100`          | How much of calendar time the strategy holds a position. Relevant for opportunity cost and margin usage                                                                                                        |

| High     | Replace `average_trade_duration_hours` with `average_trade_duration_bars` in summaries                                                                                       |
| High     | Fix `sortino_ratio` denominator — divide squared negative deviations by N (all trades), not N (negative trades only)                                                         |
| High     | Replace `total_return_percentage_of_contract` with a time/frequency-normalised version for cross-interval ranking (or clearly label it as "not comparable across intervals") |

| Medium   | Change Sharpe/Sortino `ddof` from 0 to 1                                                                                                                                     |
| Medium   | Raise `MIN_RETURNS_FOR_VAR` from 5 to 30+ for VaR/ES to have any statistical meaning                                                                                         |
| Medium   | Surface `return_percentage_of_margin` in summary metrics output                                                                                                              |
| Medium   | Add `win_loss_ratio` and `max_consecutive_losses`                                                                                                                            |
